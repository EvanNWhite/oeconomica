# Topic Starter: Applied Microeconomics (general)

## Relevant skill modules
- All four skills modules are broadly applicable here — this is the most
  general category
- `worked-examples/wages-by-education/` is itself an applied-micro example
  (labor economics / returns to education) and is a good template to
  adapt directly

## Dataset ideas
- Wage/labor data: Current Population Survey (CPS), Bureau of Labor
  Statistics
- Energy/utilities: EIA (U.S. Energy Information Administration) —
  generation mix, prices, consumption by state
- Kaggle: broad — search by your specific sub-interest (e.g., "wages,"
  "electricity," "agriculture," "healthcare pricing")

## Example research questions
- How has a wage premium (by education, by industry, by region) changed
  over time? (see the wages-by-education worked example)
- Do states with faster growth in a demand proxy (e.g., commercial
  electricity sales, as a proxy for data-center growth) see higher prices
  or a shift in supply mix over time?
- How does a specific market's price respond to a documented supply or
  demand shock?

## Note
Applied micro projects are usually the most flexible — pick a dataset in
whatever sub-area interests your group, then follow the standard workflow:
`01-data-cleaning` → `02-data-analysis` → `03-visualization` →
`04-writing-up-results`. The wages-by-education example is a good template
to copy and adapt column names/questions from directly.
